    How to use ⏱️

    Tracking your day

    Tap Clock In to start working, Clock Out to stop. The big number is today's total, updating live while you're clocked in. Clock in and out as many times as you like — breaks are just gaps between sessions.

    Adding or fixing time

    • Add manually: pick a date, a start and end time, then + Add — handy for a day you forgot to track.
    • Edit: tap a session's times to change them.
    • Delete: the ✕ next to a session (or a whole day in History).
    • Overlaps: if a new/edited session overlaps another, you'll be asked to merge them or keep both.
    • Forgot to clock out? A session left running overnight is automatically closed at the end of that day (marked auto-closed), so it never bleeds into the next day.

    History & grouping

    History collapses progressively as time passes: this week shows one line per day, earlier this month groups into weeks, and older months group into a single line. Tap any row to expand it down to the individual sessions.

    Hours format: h:m vs 0.00

    The toggle at the top of History switches how totals read:

    • h:m — hours and minutes, e.g. 7h 45m (exact).
    • 0.00 — decimal hours rounded to the nearest quarter (0.25 = 15 min), e.g. 7.75. Ideal for pasting into a timesheet.

    How rounding adds up: in decimal mode each day is rounded to a quarter-hour first, then weeks and months are the sum of those rounded days — so the numbers always reconcile (a week equals its days added up). Individual sessions always stay in h:m. Because each day is rounded, a long period's decimal total can differ by a few minutes from the raw tracked time — that's expected, and matches how per-day timesheet entries work.

    Where your data lives

    Everything is stored locally in this browser (using localStorage) — nothing is sent anywhere, there's no account, and no sync. That means:

    • Your data stays on this device & this browser only. A different browser or device starts empty.
    • Clearing your browser's site data (or "clear cookies/storage") will erase it.
    • Use Export CSV for a backup or to feed another tool — one row per day with decimal hours.

    Install on your phone

    This is a PWA: open it in your mobile browser and choose Add to Home Screen. It then launches full-screen like a native app and works offline.

    Dates & times

    All dates and clock times follow your browser/system locale automatically — no setting to configure. (So the date order and 12h/24h clock match your device.)
